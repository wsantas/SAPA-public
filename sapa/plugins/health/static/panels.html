Weekly Training Schedule

Monday - Strength A (Push/Pull)

45 min

Upper body focus - build strength for striking and grappling

Pullups4 x max
Ring Dips4 x 8-12
Rows (ring or barbell)4 x 8
OHP or Push Press4 x 6
Face Pulls3 x 15
Farmers Carry3 x 40m

Tuesday - MMA Class

60-90 min (PM)
Optional AM Session (WFH advantage)
20-30 min strength primer OR fasted cardio. Leaves 8+ hours recovery before class.
Option A: Upper body pump (3x10 pushups, pullups, dips)
Option B: 20 min fasted shadow + mobility

PM: Class with the kids - striking, grappling, conditioning

• Follow class curriculum
• Push intensity during sparring rounds
• Work technique with the boys when possible

Wednesday - Strength B (Legs/Core)

45 min

Lower body power - explosiveness for takedowns and kicks. Mid-week between MMA sessions.

Goblet or Front Squat4 x 8
Romanian Deadlift4 x 8
Bulgarian Split Squat3 x 10/leg
Hip Thrust3 x 12
Hanging Leg Raise3 x 10
Pallof Press3 x 10/side
Finish with: 10-15 min mobility (hips, shoulders, thoracic) + foam rolling

Thursday - MMA Class

60-90 min (PM)
Optional AM Session
Best day for 2-a-day since you're recovered from Tuesday.
Option A: 20-30 min conditioning (intervals, shadow rounds)
Option B: Lower body primer (goblet squats, lunges, hip work)

PM: Second class of the week - push hard

• Focus on cardio - don't hold back in conditioning
• Apply techniques learned Tuesday
• This is your main cardio session for the week

Friday - Rest / Recovery

Recovery

Full rest after Thursday MMA. Let the nervous system reset before Saturday class.

• Light walking, stretching, foam rolling only
• Mobility work if feeling stiff (hips, shoulders, thoracic)
• Prioritize sleep - early to bed, prep for Saturday AM
• Nutrition: quality fuel, hydration, don't skimp on protein

Saturday - MMA Class (AM) + Rest

60-90 min (AM)

Third MMA session of the week. Class in the morning, then shut it down.

• Follow class curriculum - striking, grappling, conditioning
• Push intensity - this is your third session, body is primed
• Work with the boys when possible
After class: Full recovery mode
Rest of Saturday is off. Good meal, hydrate, family time. No training. Let the week's work settle.

Sunday - Conditioning / Skill Work

45-60 min

Saturday afternoon recovery in the bank. Choose based on what the body needs.

Option A: Conditioning
• 5-round MMA circuit
• Shadow + rings + sprawls
• Push cardio ceiling
Option B: Skill Work
• Technical shadowboxing
• BJJ solo drills
• Movement flow + mobility
Every 4th Week - Deload

Cut volume by 50%. Same exercises, fewer sets. Focus on technique and recovery. This is when your body actually adapts and gets stronger.

Breathing Protocol Details

Morning Boot Sequence (6-7 AM)

1. Cyclic Sighing - 5 min
Double inhale through nose (fill lungs, then top off), long slow exhale through mouth. Activates parasympathetic dominance. Proven to lower cortisol more effectively than box breathing or meditation (Huberman/Stanford 2023).
2. Resonance Breathing - 5 min
5.5 sec inhale, 5.5 sec exhale through nose. ~5.5 breaths/min. Maximizes HRV by syncing respiratory and cardiac cycles. This is your daily vagal tone calibration.
3. Cold Face Splash
Cold water on face activates the diving reflex - instant vagal brake. Pairs with breathing to lock in parasympathetic tone.

Desk Protocol (Work Hours)

Default mode during coding. Nasal breathing is non-negotiable.

Default: Nasal Only
Mouth closed, tongue on palate. Produces nitric oxide (vasodilator), filters air, maintains CO2 tolerance. If you catch yourself mouth breathing, you're either too stressed or the room is too warm.
Pre-Focus Block: 2 min Resonance
Before starting a deep work block, 10-12 breaths at 5.5/5.5. Shifts brain into alpha-dominant state. Better than caffeine for sustained attention.
Ultradian Break (every 90 min): 10 Resonance Breaths + Movement Snack
90-min ultradian rhythm is real. Performance drops after 90 min regardless of caffeine. 10 breaths + standing reset = full cognitive refresh.
Frustration/Stuck: Mouth Breathing Check
When stuck on a bug, first check: are you mouth breathing? If yes, close mouth, take 10 slow nasal breaths at 4/7 cadence. Right-shifts the dissociation curve, increases brain O2 delivery. Insight usually arrives at minute 2-3.
Energy Dip: Extended Exhale 3 min
4 sec inhale, 8 sec exhale nasal. Parasympathetic reset without drowsiness. Better than a second coffee at 2 PM.
Bohr Effect in practice: Higher CO2 tolerance from nasal breathing → hemoglobin releases O2 more efficiently to tissues → your brain gets more oxygen during coding sessions. Mouth breathing dumps CO2 and paradoxically reduces brain oxygenation.

Post-Training Protocol

Shift from fight-or-flight back to recovery as fast as possible. The faster you downregulate, the faster you recover.

Immediately post-training: Cyclic Sighing 3-5 min
Double inhale + long exhale. Fastest known method to shift from sympathetic to parasympathetic. Do this before you even leave the gym.
Drive home: Nasal only
Resist the urge to mouth breathe after hard training. Keeps CO2 levels elevated, continues recovery signaling.
At home: Extended Exhale 5 min
4 sec in, 8 sec out. Deep parasympathetic lock. Pair with light stretching or foam rolling.

Pre-Sleep Protocol

Bridge from waking to sleep. Goal: arrive at bed already parasympathetic-dominant.

8:30 PM: Extended Exhale 5 min
4 sec in, 8 sec out nasal. This is your shutdown signal. Same time every night - the consistency matters as much as the breathing itself.
Bed: Mouth tape
Forces nasal breathing during sleep. Maintains CO2, produces NO, prevents dry mouth/snoring.
Position: Left side preferred
Stomach anatomy positions the gastric inlet above gastric acid when lying on the left. Combined with mouth tape = best overnight
Quick Reference - Breathing Cadences
Resonance: 5.5 in / 5.5 out (nasal)
Cyclic Sighing: double inhale / long exhale (mouth)
Extended Exhale: 4 in / 8 out (nasal)
Stuck/Focus: 4 in / 7 out (nasal)
