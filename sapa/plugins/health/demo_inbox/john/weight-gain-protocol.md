# Lean Weight Gain Protocol for Hardgainers

A practical, evidence-based protocol for adding high-quality mass without sacrificing metabolic health. This is built around progressive overload, nutrition timing, and recovery — not dirty bulking.

## The Core Principle

Lean weight gain is a training + nutrition + recovery problem, solved simultaneously. You cannot out-eat bad programming, and you cannot out-lift bad nutrition. Target a modest caloric surplus of 250–400 calories above maintenance — enough to fuel hypertrophy without ballooning body fat.

## Nutrition Targets

- **Protein:** 0.8–1.0g per pound of bodyweight daily. For a 170lb male: 135–170g/day.
- **Carbohydrates:** 2.0–3.0g per pound. Rice, potatoes, oats, fruit — prioritize whole foods.
- **Fats:** 0.4–0.5g per pound. Organ meats, egg yolks, olive oil, grass-fed butter.
- **Calories:** Maintenance + 300 to start. Adjust weekly based on scale trend.
- **Meal timing:** 4–6 meals, protein in each. Nutrient timing around training matters more at the edges than the middle.

### Sample Day

- **Breakfast:** 4 whole eggs, 1 cup oats with banana, bone broth
- **Mid-morning:** Greek yogurt with honey and collagen, handful of nuts
- **Lunch:** 8oz grass-fed beef, rice, roasted vegetables, avocado
- **Pre-workout:** Fruit and coffee
- **Post-workout:** Whey protein, rice cakes with honey
- **Dinner:** 6oz salmon, sweet potato, greens with olive oil
- **Before bed:** Cottage cheese or casein protein

## Training — Compound Movements First

Progressive overload on compound lifts is the engine of hypertrophy. Run a 4-day upper/lower split:

- **Monday (Lower):** Squat 5x5, RDL 3x8, walking lunges 3x10, calves
- **Tuesday (Upper):** Bench press 5x5, weighted pull-ups 4x6, overhead press 3x8, rows 3x10
- **Thursday (Lower):** Deadlift 5x3, front squat 3x6, hip hinge work, core stability
- **Friday (Upper):** Incline bench 4x6, chin-ups 4x8, dips 3x10, curls and triceps

Add 2.5–5 lbs per week on main lifts until stalled. Periodization matters: run a deload week every 6–8 weeks to allow tissue healing and nervous system recovery.

## Recovery is Non-Negotiable

- **Sleep:** 8–9 hours. Deep sleep drives growth hormone release. Track HRV and readiness scores if you have a wearable.
- **Active recovery:** Walks, mobility work, zone 2 cardio 1–2x/week.
- **Foam rolling and stretching** after training sessions.
- **Stress management:** Chronic cortisol blocks hypertrophy. Protect sleep and reduce inflammation.

## Supplements Worth It

- **Creatine monohydrate:** 5g daily, forever. Best-studied supplement in existence.
- **Whey protein:** Convenience, hits protein targets on busy days.
- **Collagen peptides:** Tissue repair, joint and tendon support.
- **Vitamin D3 + K2:** Hormone optimization, bone health.
- **Magnesium glycinate:** Sleep quality, muscle relaxation.
- **Electrolytes:** Especially if sweating in training.

## Tracking

- Weigh yourself daily, same conditions. Track the weekly average, not the daily number.
- Waist measurement weekly. If waist grows faster than bodyweight, pull calories.
- Lift numbers in a log. Progressive overload requires tracking.
- Photos every 4 weeks.

## Common Mistakes

- **Eating "clean" but too little.** Whole foods are great but you still need the calories.
- **Program hopping.** Pick a program, run it 12+ weeks.
- **Skipping legs.** Leg training drives full-body hormonal response.
- **Cardio overdose.** Some zone 2 is fine. HIIT 5x/week will stall mass gain.
- **Ignoring recovery.** More training is not better. Better recovery is better.

## Timeline Expectations

Realistic lean mass gain: 1–2 lbs/month for intermediate lifters, 0.5–1 lb/month for advanced. Anything faster is mostly fat and water. Patience compounds.
